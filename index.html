How to Make Her Happy (State Lines Edition)

By Niv

A highly guarded, fiercely tested recipe for sustaining the most brilliant girl you know across states. Best served daily via text, voice notes, and the occasional glorious FaceTime, with a side of witty banter and zero bullshit.

1,114
Yield: 1 radiant Aaradhya.
Distance: Several states apart.
Time: 5 minutes of prep, plus a lifetime of delightful digital maintenance until the gap closes.
After months of rigorous R&D—and placing entirely too much trust in our respective cellular networks—I’ve perfected the ultimate recipe for Aaradhya. Forget physical truffles and caviar; this girl operates on a complex blend of searing intellect, a poetic soul, and carbonated beverages. When you live states away and have only FaceTimed once (a legendary occasion, for the record), the dish requires precision, elite reading comprehension, and knowing exactly when to deploy a DoorDash surprise to her exact coordinates.

Preparation (The Method)
Step 1 Clear the digital counter. When you're texting or calling, actually be there. Don't half-watch a movie in the background. She's a genius; she can sense distracted typing from hundreds of miles away.
Step 2 Simmer the conversation. Let her switch wildly from cellular biology to expressing her love to you. Your job is to keep up, ask the right questions, and send the absolute perfect reaction meme when words fail.
Step 3 The Art of the Remote Garnish. You can't physically hand her a drink, so you must become a master of digital logistics. A surprise delivery of ice cream hitting her doorstep right when she craves it is Michelin-star romance.
Step 4 Fold in the contradictions. Gently mix her ruthless ambition with her wildly soft heart. Remind her that it's okay to be both. She doesn't have to be "on" for you; she just has to be herself.
Step 5 Plate for the future. Long distance is just an extended marinating process. Keep the end-goal in sight. Remind her often that the state lines are temporary, but she is permanent. Garnish every night with a goodnight text she can wake up to.
Tips from the Kitchen
Patience is Key: We've only FaceTimed once, which makes the moments we *do* connect even sweeter. Quality over quantity. Let the tension build; it makes the dish better.
Substitutions: There is absolutely NO substitution for the Diet Coke. Don't even try ordering her a Pepsi. It's a dealbreaker.
Recipe Notes

Early iterations of this recipe involved me trying way too hard to orchestrate massive, impressive gestures. Turns out, she didn't need a showman; she just needed me, consistently showing up in her notifications and her life.

The real breakthrough happened when I realized I don't need to overcompensate for the miles between our states. I just need to create a safe space for her to be exactly who she is. I'm just here to be the very lucky sous-chef in her spectacular life, no matter the geography.

How to Make Her Feel Loved (Across State Lines)

Yield: Deep security • Active time: 5 minutes of focused texting
Key Ingredients
  • Hyper-specific compliments ("Your brain is terrifyingly beautiful")
  • Remembering that random event she muttered about last Tuesday

Method
  • Notice the little things she texts you. Write them down if you have to.
  • Remind her she's brilliant, not just beautiful.
  • Follow through. Talk is cheap, but consistency across timezones is gold.
Warning: She's a genius, so she can spot an automated "good morning" text from a mile away. Sincerity is your only option.

How to Help Her Relax (Via Screen)

Time: Varies wildly based on her current stubbornness level
Key Ingredients
  • Firm permission to close her laptop
  • An order of comfort snacks sent to her place
  • A perfectly timed, distraction-free phone call
Method
  • Gently but firmly text her that the world won't end if she takes a nap.
  • Tell her to put the phone on DND (except for you, obviously).
  • Create a 100% guilt-free, low-stakes digital environment.
High-achievers are notoriously bad at resting. You can't physically take the textbook away, so you have to use logic, love, and food bribery.
